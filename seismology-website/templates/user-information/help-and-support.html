What kind of files does the site uses ?

The site uses MiniSEED or MSEED binary format files that store and exchange digital seismic waveform data.

What tools are used to build this website ?

The technology used to build this website consists of tools that have to do with the front-end side such as HTML, CSS, Javascript and tools related to the front-end side or server side that we use the Flask Python library.

What is the site about ?

The site is about earthquake related concepts and tools. Our platform is thoughtfully designed to serve individuals from various backgrounds and levels of knowledge. Here, we're passionate about making earthquake knowledge accessible to everyone. Our mission is to empower you with insights and resources that span from the basics to more advanced insights. Our collection includes a wide array of articles and tools carefully crafted to ensure that regardless of your familiarity with the topic, you'll find something valuable and engaging.

How do I navigate through the website?

We've organized our content into two main categories: articles and interactive tools. Dive into our articles to gain deeper insights into various seismological concepts, and don't miss out on our interactive tools that let you explore and apply different seismological computations.

Will the site be regularly updated with new content?

Yes, we are committed to providing you with a dynamic and evolving resource. Our team is dedicated to regularly updating the site with fresh articles, tools, and the latest insights in the field of seismology. Be sure to check back often to discover new additions that will continue to enrich your understanding and exploration of earthquakes.
